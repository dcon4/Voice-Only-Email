# VoiceGmail — User Instructions

VoiceGmail is a completely hands-free email app designed for totally blind users.
You never need to touch the screen. Every action is done by voice.

---

## Getting started

1. Open the app. It will greet you and tell you how many emails you have.
2. After every spoken message, the microphone opens automatically.
3. Speak your command. You do not need to press any button first.

---

## Waking the app from the lock screen

Press the **power button** to wake your device.  
VoiceGmail will appear on screen and say **"VoiceGmail ready — say a command"**.

You do not need to unlock your phone. The app works fully over the lock screen.

---

## Reading email

| Say this              | What happens                                        |
|-----------------------|-----------------------------------------------------|
| **read**              | Reads the current email in full                     |
| **next**              | Moves to the next email and announces sender/subject|
| **previous**          | Moves to the previous email                         |
| **repeat**            | Reads the current email again from the beginning    |
| **read unread**       | Jumps to your first unread message                  |

---

## Replying and composing

| Say this      | What happens                                                  |
|---------------|---------------------------------------------------------------|
| **reply**     | Opens compose screen pre-filled with the sender's address     |
| **compose**   | Opens a blank compose screen                                  |
| **send**      | Sends the email you have composed                             |
| **read back** | Reads back what you have written so far                       |
| **cancel**    | Discards the current email without sending                    |

When composing, the app guides you step by step:
- First it asks for the **recipient's email address** — speak it aloud.
- Then it asks for the **subject** — speak it aloud.
- Then it asks for the **body** — speak your message.
- Say **"send"** when you are ready to send.

---

## Managing email

| Say this           | What happens                                                |
|--------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------|
| **delete**         | Asks you to confirm, then moves email to Trash             |
| **yes**            | Confirms a deletion or other action                         |
| **cancel**         | Cancels without deleting                                    |
| **mark as read**   | Clears the unread badge on the current email                |
| **forward**        | Asks for a recipient address, then forwards the email       |
| **refresh**        | Reloads your inbox                                          |

---

## Searching

| Say this            | What happens                              |
|---------------------|-------------------------------------------|
| **search**          | Opens a search prompt                     |
| **find**            | Same as search                            |

After saying "search", describe what you want:

- *"emails from David"*
- *"subject meeting notes"*
- *"unread emails"*
- *"emails about the invoice"*

In search results, use **next**, **previous**, **read**, **reply**, and **delete** exactly as in your inbox.

---

## Attachments

| Say this                    | What happens                                              |
|-----------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------|
| **list attachments**        | Reads out the names of all files attached to the email    |
| **read attachment one**     | Reads the first attachment aloud                          |
| **read attachment two**     | Reads the second attachment aloud                         |

Supported file types: **PDF**, **Word (.docx)**, **plain text (.txt)**.

---

## Voice and speech settings

Say **"voice settings"** to change the text-to-speech engine or voice.

The app will:
1. List all TTS engines installed on your device (e.g. Google TTS, Samsung TTS).
2. Ask you to say a number to choose one.
3. List available voices for that engine (e.g. "English UK female", "English US male").
4. Ask you to say a number to choose one.
5. Test the new voice by saying "Hello, this is your new voice."

Your choice is saved and used every time the app starts.

To install more voices: go to **Settings → Accessibility → Text-to-Speech** on your device
and download additional language packs from your chosen TTS engine.

## Help

| Say this         | What happens                              |
|------------------|-------------------------------------------|
| **help**         | Lists all available commands briefly      |
| **instructions** | Plays this guide as spoken audio, section by section |

When listening to instructions, say:
- **next** — go to the next section
- **previous** — go back one section
- **repeat** — hear the current section again
- any command — exit instructions and run that command

## Tips for blind users

- **You never need to look at the screen.** Every action has spoken feedback.
- **The app waits for you.** After it finishes speaking, the mic opens. Take your time.
- **If the mic misses you**, say "repeat" to hear the last message again, then speak your command.
- **To stop mid-speech**, you can press the volume button — then say your command.
- **For multiple emails**, say "next" repeatedly to scan subject lines before deciding to read.
- **The greeting changes by time of day** — "Good morning", "Good afternoon", "Good evening".
